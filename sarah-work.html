Clay Certifications — Launched Strong, Then Paused

We certified nearly 1,000 people across four tracks in six months. But several signals converged: the assessment was gameable (public rubric + AI meant people could reverse-engineer a passing submission), scoring didn't distinguish quality levels, and we lacked a clear north star for what the credential means to an employer. The litmus test — would Clay's own talent team hire someone because of this cert? — we couldn't answer with conviction.

What I took from it

We shipped before fully defining the bar. I'd do the demand-side work earlier next time — building backward from employer expectations, not forward from what's easiest to assess. The retro I wrote (March 2026) is structured around one question: what does it mean to be Clay Certified, and would someone hire you because of it?
